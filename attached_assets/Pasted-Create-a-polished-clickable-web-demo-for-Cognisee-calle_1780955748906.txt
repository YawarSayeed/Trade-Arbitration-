Create a polished clickable web demo for Cognisee called “Epistemic Foraging for International Trade Dispute Arbitration.”

Context:
This is a concept demo for law firms handling complex cross-border trade disputes. The demo should show how Cognisee helps arbitration teams explore venue strategy by testing the same dispute across four major arbitration-friendly legal seats: New York, London, Singapore, and Hong Kong.

Goal:
Build an interactive clickable prototype that demonstrates how a legal team can evaluate risk, win probability, precedent fit, procedural friction, enforcement confidence, evidentiary burden, and strategic tradeoffs across jurisdictions. This is not a legal advice tool. It is a decision-intelligence demo showing how expert judgment, case history, tacit legal knowledge, and jurisdictional patterns can support better venue selection.

Core concept:
The demo should show “epistemic foraging,” meaning the user can explore evidence, assumptions, precedent clusters, expert judgment signals, counterarguments, uncertainty, and decision paths rather than receiving a simple answer.

Design style:
Use a premium enterprise SaaS interface. Dark institutional theme with deep green, charcoal, off-white text, and subtle gold accents. It should feel like a high-stakes legal strategy cockpit for a global law firm. Keep it clean, serious, and boardroom-ready.
